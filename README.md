# Baseline
## Inspiration
Within our modern society, mental disability is ubiquitous, with around 1 in 4 Americans struggling with a condition of some sort. Although every case is highly personal, research shows many of those who struggle through these disabilities tackle anxiety and/or issues in social settings; especially including alexithymia, the inability to recognise facial expressions or other social cues connected to emotion. Thus, our team designed BASELINE as a means to provide a homebase for mental health regulation, offering guided breathing services, in addition to expression recognition technology. 


## What it does
BASELINE consists of two main technologies: a facial recognition software, and an assistive device to regulate breathing. On the website, BASELINE analyzes facial cues in real time and notifies users of their precise emotional implications. The site also includes a simple and intuitive way to log emotions, allowing the user a better understanding of their own symptoms and thus, a better path to discovering coping mechanisms. In addition, BASELINE includes a wearable breathing rate detector and a guided breathing device. Both connect back to the website, where the user’s breathing is visually represented as a waveform graph, with the guided device shown similarly for easy comparison. 
## How we built it
As a group we divided the technical work into three main facets: the wearable device, the guided box breathing tool, and the BASELINE website. Both the wearable and the box breathing device was created through the use of an arduino breadboard. For the wearable, an ultrasonic sensor was utilized to observe the rate in which the user’s chest rises (thus representing breathing rate.) The box breathing device used a series of lights, each on for around one second to guide users to breathing rate of 5 breaths per minute.

## What's next for our project
After further development, we see BASELINE growing into a tool used as a primary tool to combat the struggles of Americans with conditions like Anxiety, Autism, PTSD, among others. Our team is excited by possibilities of an even more compact wearable device that can perform an even more complex analysis of hormonal increases attributed to mood. Having something like BASELINE, a true homebase for medical and written accounts of mental health can be life changing for those who struggle. We see the users of BASELINE gaining great emotional intelligence from the use of our project, and better managing what may have once limited them.
